Cultural beauty refers to the standards, ideals, and practices of attractiveness

are specific to a particular culture or group of people.

Beauty standards can vary widely from one culture to another, influenced by a variety of factors

including history, geography, religion, social norms, and individual preferences.

Diversity of Standards: Different cultures have diverse notions of what is considered beautiful.

For example, in some cultures, fair skin might be highly prized, while in others, a tanned complexion is seen as more attractive.

cultural art

Cultural art encompasses a wide range of creative expressions produced by various societies around the world. It reflects the values, beliefs, traditions, and aesthetics of a particular culture or group of people. This can include visual arts (such as painting, sculpture, and photography), performing arts (like dance, theater, and music), literature, architecture, and other forms of creative expression.
